## Representative tool

Atomic Fact Decomposition & Support Ratio Scoring

## Tool explanation (full)

This tool automatically segments long texts generated by models (e.g., a 200-word popular-science paragraph) into semantic atomic facts, where each atomic fact must be independently verifiable as true, false, or unverifiable by a single trusted knowledge source (e.g., Wikipedia, PubMed); it then invokes an external retriever (e.g., BM25+BERT) to match supporting evidence and computes “number of supported atomic facts / total number of atomic facts” as the FActScore. Fundamentally, it transforms overall text credibility into an auditable, fine-grained support ratio.

Extraction basis: Primarily drawn from the Benchmark diagnostic table notes: the note explicitly states “Type: Long-text factual accuracy evaluation” and “splitting output into atomic facts provides finer granularity than binary correctness”, and all three scoring items in strong_rubric_evidence are rated 5, repeatedly emphasizing “splitting long text into verifiable atomic facts and computing the support ratio”, confirming that this decomposition and scoring mechanism constitutes its signature contribution.

## What it improves vs. baseline

Compared with traditional holistic text accuracy metrics (e.g., ROUGE-L matching) or binary factual correctness judgments, this tool provides a continuous, decomposable, and traceable factualness metric, enabling error localization to specific sentence constituents—for instance, the statement “Newton discovered gravity” is correctly decomposed into the atomic facts “Newton proposed the law of universal gravitation” and “Gravity was discovered by Newton”—thus supporting attribution analysis and targeted improvement.

## Adaptation example

In the financial research report summarization task, automatically decompose the model’s output of industry trend analysis (e.g., “New energy vehicle penetration rate will reach 45% by 2025”) into atomic facts (e.g., “Target NEV penetration rate for 2025”, “This value originates from MIIT planning documents”), and verify the support rate against the CSRC filing database—replacing coarse-grained, manual full-report review.
